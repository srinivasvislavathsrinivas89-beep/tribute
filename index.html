Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Father of the Nation, India (1869 - 1948)

About Gandhiji

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a leader of India’s non-violent independence movement against British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world through his philosophy of truth and non-violence (Satyagraha and Ahimsa).

Key Events in His Life

  • 1869: Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India
  • 1893: Fought against racial discrimination in South Africa
  • 1915: Returned to India and joined the independence movement
  • 1930: Led the Salt March against British salt laws
  • 1947: India achieved independence
  • 1948: Assassinated in New Delhi

Legacy

Gandhi’s life and teachings continue to inspire generations. His message of peace and non-violence is more relevant than ever in today’s world.
